Pre-Colonial Philippine Culture

A glimpse into the rich history and traditions

Traditions & Heritage

The Philippines before the arrival of colonial powers had a vibrant culture influenced by a diverse mix of indigenous traditions. People lived in small, self-sustained communities governed by a local chieftain, known as a Datu. The pre-colonial Filipinos were skilled in various arts such as weaving, pottery, and metallurgy. They also had a unique system of writing known as Baybayin.

Language & Literature

Baybayin was the writing system used in the Philippines before Spanish colonization. It was an abugida (syllabic alphabet) and was primarily used for poetry and storytelling. Oral tradition was a significant aspect of pre-colonial Filipino culture, and stories were passed down through generations through chants, songs, and epic poems like the *Hinilawod*.

Religion & Beliefs

Filipinos before colonization practiced animism and had a rich pantheon of gods, goddesses, and spirits. They believed in the power of nature and its connection to daily life, from their agricultural practices to their rituals in honoring ancestors.
